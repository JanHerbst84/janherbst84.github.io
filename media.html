Heaviness in Metal Music Production (HiMMP)

Welcome to the media section of Heaviness in Metal Music Production (HiMMP), a research project funded by the Arts and Humanities Research Council (AHRC) between 2020 and 2024 and hosted at the University of Huddersfield. HiMMP explored how heaviness is created and controlled in metal music production through expert interviews, producer showcases, and in-depth analyses.

Discover insights from some of the leading metal producers, including Jens Bogren, Fredrik Nordström, and Andrew Scheps, and more, as they discuss their unique approaches to mixing metal. Watch the videos below or explore the archive on our YouTube channel.

For more details about the project, visit the official HiMMP website.

Video Trailer HiMMP

What makes (metal) music "heavy"? In these exclusive interviews, leading metal producers explain their concept of heaviness in metal music production. From sonic weight and distortion to rhythmic and emotional impact, each expert offers unique perspectives on what defines heaviness and how it can be shaped in the studio.






How do different producers approach mixing the same metal track? Below, eight renowned metal producers share their insights into mixing 'In Solitude', offering a rare glimpse into their creative decisions, techniques, and workflows.

Each video provides a detailed breakdown of the producer's mix, while the accompanying audio files allow those interested to compare their individual mixes side by side. Explore the impact of different mixing styles on heaviness, clarity, and cohesion.
