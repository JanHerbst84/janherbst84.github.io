Popular Music Studies

My research in popular music studies is extensive, with a particular focus on metal music as a vibrant subculture. I have explored topics ranging from the socio-cultural analysis of music scenes to the economic realities of musicians and songwriters. My work has been published in journals such as Popular Music and Society, Popular Music, Popular Music Studies, and Rock Music Studies.

Music Production and Technology

I have a strong interest in the technological aspects of music production. My research in this area examines the impact of technology on musical creativity, the conventions of music production, and the cultural significance of music technology. I have published on these topics in journals like the Journal of Popular Music Studies, Metal Music Studies, and El Oido Pensante.

Music Psychology

My work in music psychology investigates the perceptual and cognitive aspects of music. I have conducted studies on topics such as the perception of 'heaviness' in music, the influence of distortion on harmony, and the acoustical properties of musical instruments. My research in this area has been published in journals like Music Perception, Samples, and the Journal of the German Society for Music Psychology.
